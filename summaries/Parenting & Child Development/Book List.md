# Parenting & Child Development — Book List

## Immediate Priority (Toddler Stage)

| # | Title | Author | Why |
|---|-------|--------|-----|
| 1 | **The Whole-Brain Child** | Daniel J. Siegel & Tina Payne Bryson | Neuroscience-based framework for understanding how a child's brain develops — the single most referenced parenting book |
| 2 | **No-Drama Discipline** | Daniel J. Siegel & Tina Payne Bryson | Companion to the above — handles tantrums, boundaries, and discipline without shame |
| 3 | **The Montessori Toddler** | Simone Davies | Practical Montessori approach specifically for the 1-3 age range |
| 4 | **No Bad Kids** | Janet Lansbury | Short, direct book on toddler discipline using the RIE (respectful parenting) approach |
| 5 | **Hunt, Gather, Parent** | Michaeleen Doucleff | What indigenous cultures worldwide can teach us — challenges a lot of Western parenting assumptions |

## Foundational Thinking (Read Anytime)

| # | Title | Author | Why |
|---|-------|--------|-----|
| 6 | **Brain Rules for Baby** | John Medina | Neuroscience of child development 0-5, very practical |
| 7 | **The Gardener and the Carpenter** | Alison Gopnik | Reframes parenting from "shaping a child" to "creating conditions for growth" — philosophy-shifting |
| 8 | **Unconditional Parenting** | Alfie Kohn | The case against rewards/punishments — challenges conventional approaches |
| 9 | **Parenting from the Inside Out** | Daniel J. Siegel | How your own childhood wiring affects your parenting — pairs well with the self-mastery shelf |
| 10 | **Cribsheet** | Emily Oster | Data-driven answers to every parenting decision (sleep training, breastfeeding, screen time, etc.) |

## Communication & Connection (Ages 2+)

| # | Title | Author | Why |
|---|-------|--------|-----|
| 11 | **How to Talk So Little Kids Will Listen** | Joanna Faber & Julie King | The practical bible for parent-child communication ages 2-7 |
| 12 | **The Danish Way of Parenting** | Jessica Joelle Alexander | Why Danish kids consistently rank among the happiest — emphasises play, empathy, reframing |
| 13 | **Simplicity Parenting** | Kim John Payne | Reducing overwhelm, toys, schedules — creating calm for both parent and child |

## Long Game (Worth Having on the Shelf)

| # | Title | Author | Why |
|---|-------|--------|-----|
| 14 | **How to Talk So Kids Will Listen** | Adele Faber & Elaine Mazlish | The original — covers ages 7+ where the "Little Kids" version leaves off |
| 15 | **The Self-Driven Child** | William Stixrud & Ned Johnson | Autonomy and motivation — most relevant from age 5+ but the framework is worth knowing early |
